What Technology is Used in Sensible ?

Our mission is to deliver fast, reliable air‑quality data in for quick actions and better air quality for everyone.

Thus, Sensible is packed with Electrochemical sensors react with gases like CO and ozone for instant readings, while catalytic sensors detect combustible fumes by measuring tiny heat changes. Optical sensors—including NDIR for accurate CO₂ tracking and metal oxide semiconductors for VOC detection—keep tabs on volatile pollutants. A built-in spectroscopy module then uses light signatures to spot even trace chemicals. The result is a complete, up-to-the-minute view of your indoor air—no jargon, just actionable insights so you can breathe easy.

Sensible uses advanced sensor technologies like electrochemical, catalytic, optical (NDIR, PID, metal oxide semiconductors), and spectroscopy to provide accurate, real-time air quality data.

These technologies work in harmony to ensure that the data you receive is both reliable and actionable, helping you maintain cleaner, healthier indoor air.
